What? ASE

Dug from the internet pile, ready for consumption. Yummy!

Before video replays, we had input files (inp). Small files you could easily send to others online. A popular option given that YouTube didn't exist (or barely got started and looked like shit) and downloading video took forever. You'd run those inp files in MAME and it would play back the inputs of the player. A good way to share your replays for the time.

Over 15 years later, a lot of these replays have not been archived into video form and run on decade old versions of MAME. Average Superplay Enjoyers is a project to make these replays more pubicly available, with guest commentators to explain the mechanics and strategies of the games.
